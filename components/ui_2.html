Old Brand Redesign

  • by:Avery Chen
  • date:Jun, 2017
  • Web Design, Graphic Design, Photoshop, Illustrator
This program is about designing an old brand that seeks to rebrand itself. The brief asks for some new logo and banner to create a brand-new sister brand with a higher price range for similar product lines and types.

Visual Transformation for Original Brand

The following are two new logos for the brand. One is for B2B e-commerce selling skincare products, and the other is for a newly opened Spa place.  
Purple is the brand’s original color scheme, and I chose to continue using this color not to evoke a sense of unfamiliarity among the frequent customers. I brought together butterflies and female faces because butterflies are the mascot for the original brand.
Another feature is silhouettes of Japanese ladies. Feminine faces are a common element found in the sister brand and the Spa place. I chose the bright red on the Japanese national flag to echo the purple-themed webpages but different enough to pop out. The bright red dot logo becomes the symbol that best represents Japan – the sun.

New Sister Brand

Style requirement: Featuring anime heroines.

Target customers: Middle-aged women.

Special requirements: Must include a four-leaf clover keychain (the main supplier’s logo)

Visual Transformation for Original Brand

Color scheme: Grayish Morandi color palette. All the females are created in powdery colors with some red highlights matched with gold to convey a sense of high-end sophistication.

Featuring female anime characters who also give off an air of sophistication. The design is presented in a more realistic style and models after traditional Japanese dress of female adults.
